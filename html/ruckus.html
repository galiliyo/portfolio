Who?

Ruckus Wireless A web portal and mobile app for an industry leader in wired and wireless networking equipment. Cloud Wi-Fi enables any organization with any level of IT resources to quickly and easily set up, monitor and manage a campus or multi-site WLAN of any size

My Role

As an ongoing project at 5ive, I was charged with the mobile app and was involved from concept to launch. Two and a half years, a couple of major releases and more than 50 screens later we have a working app that gained good traction.

My responsibilities included finding UX solutions for new features, as well as migrating existing features from the desktop version to mobile. We utilized an Agile methodology in close contact with product owners, internal Ruckus UX and the int’l development team. I designed the UX, built and presented prototypes, oversaw cohesive design of the UI conforming to the styleguide we put together and supported ongoing development.

Case in Point

The app's purpose is for installing and monitoring distributed networks and their components. These may include hundred of routers (‘access points’) carrying different kinds of networks.

We wanted to enable users to effortlessly examine the system’s many components in different contexts. For this reason we introduced a circular navigation scheme in which each reference to an entity in the system is a link to that entity.

The user may effortlessly jump from a venue to network in that venue, to a client on that network and so on, easily switching contexts while never losing his place in the information space.
