Who?

Amdocs Engage is a customer journey and life cycle management for the Telco industry.

The system decides on the next best action to take at each touch point on the customer's journey. It does so on an individual basis in real time, using customer behavioral data from various sources and predicted patterns using state of the art AI.

Originally developed by Pontis, later acquired by Amdocs and integrated into the Amdocs aia platform

Amdocs aia is an industry-specific digital intelligence platform that implements business logic to automate service provider’s business, enabling the ‘self-driving telco’: a business that can auto-adapt, using real-time data to feed processes and take action

My Role

As UX designer, I assisted the product owners and system architects in realizing their vision for the product and was involved from concept to daily support.

I Provided UX solutions and consulted on new and existing designs, created UX deliverables and acted as liaison between the product, UI designers and development teams.

Another part of my job included creating Interactive mockups - some of which were highly complex and elaborate. The mockups were mainly done in Axure and served two main purposes:

  • As internal and external marketing tools, demonstrating the product's vision and future directions
  • As prototypes that communicate specific user stories, features and interactions to the stakeholders and development team

Case in Point

One of many features in this hyper-complex product is a Funnel that displays aggregates of the users flowing through the system. A few requirements complicated this design compared to a standard funnel:

  • The number of steps vary depending on circumstances and may be further divided into sub-steps
  • It’s required to display both who passed each step as well as the drop offs
  • In addition, it’s required to show further breakdowns of the total users for continued investigation

Our task was to display all this information without overloading the user. In order to achieve this we followed the following principles A few requirements complicated this design compared to a standard funnel:

  • Making the essential information immediately accessible
  • Using color, size and whitespace to create hierarchy
  • Displaying initial data in a clear manner while providing opportunities to drill down to see more
